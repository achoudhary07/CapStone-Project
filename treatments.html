Information about Treatments for the Diseases

A medical treatment is when you try to remedy a disease or medical condition. Treatment usually starts after a professional medical diagnosis has been given.

  • Tuberculosis
  • The usual treatment to treat TB would be prescribed antibacterial medications (antibiotics). The pescribed period to take the medication would be about 6 months.
  • Hepatitis B
  • The treatment for Hepatitis B would be antiviral medications. In more critical cases, there might be a liver transplant needed. There is no real "cure" for Hepatitis B, as in some cases it might just go away on its own. In order to prevent Hepatitis B from occurring, you should primarily take a vaccine.
  • Polio
  • There is no actual cure for polio, but there are certain meausres taken in order to make the lives of people with polio easier. Those measures include pain relievers, bed rest, and portable ventilators.
  • Shingles
  • The usual treatment to treat shingles would be to prescribe antivral medications, as well as pain relievers. Since shingles usually cause rashes and blisters, there are creams, gels, sprays, and lotions that you can apply to reduce the pain as well. To minimize of getting it, you should take a vaccine.
