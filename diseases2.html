  • Shingles
  • Shingles is a viral infection that usually causes blisters on the body. The virus that leads to the infection is called the varicella-zoster virus. In case you have had a prior case of chickenpox, that same virus will remain dormant, and might become active again later on as a case of shingles. Symptoms may include:fever, headache, pain burning, itching, red rash. . .
