Information about some Diseases

Diseases are conditions which halt normal bodily function of organisms and usually cause an exhibition of symptoms pertaining to what type of disease is causing the sickness.

  • Tuberculosis
  • Tuberculosis(TB) is a serious infection that spreads through tiny droplets that are released into the air, through coughs or sneezes. It mostly affects the lungs. TB can also occur in other organs of the body, such as the brain or spine. Symptoms include:coughing up blood, chest pain, fatigue, fever, chills, night sweats. . .

  • Hepatitis B
  • Hepatitis B is an infection that is caused by the hepatitis b virus (HBV). Some people that may be newly infected with the virus might not show apparent symptoms immediately. In case you do show symptoms, symptoms include but are not limited to:stomach pain, fatigue, nausea, and jaundice. . .
